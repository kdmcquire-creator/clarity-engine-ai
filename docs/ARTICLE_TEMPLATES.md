# Clarity Engine Article Formatting Templates

## Template 1: "Expert Comparison" (Head-to-Head Analysis)
**Best for:** Tool comparisons, platform reviews, strategy debates
**Structure:** Intro hook → fundamental differences → feature-by-feature comparison → verdict → author sign-off

### Example Structure:
- **Opening Hook** (2-3 sentences): Personal anecdote or surprising stat that frames the debate
- **The Fundamental Difference** (1 paragraph): Core distinction between the two options
- **Feature Deep Dive** (5-7 subsections): Each feature gets a short paragraph + winner declaration
- **Pricing Reality Check** (1 paragraph): Cost analysis and value proposition
- **The Verdict** (1-2 paragraphs): Clear recommendation with use cases
- **Author Sign-Off** (1 sentence): Witty, personality-driven farewell unique to writer

---

## Template 2: "Comprehensive Guide" (Educational Deep-Dive)
**Best for:** How-to guides, skill-building, foundational knowledge
**Structure:** Intro → key concepts → step-by-step breakdown → common mistakes → pro tips → conclusion

### Example Structure:
- **Why This Matters** (1 paragraph): Context and relevance
- **Core Concepts** (2-3 subsections): Define key terms and frameworks
- **Step-by-Step Breakdown** (4-6 numbered steps): Actionable process
- **Common Mistakes to Avoid** (3-4 bullet points): Pitfalls and how to sidestep them
- **Pro Tips from the Field** (3-4 subsections): Advanced tactics and insider knowledge
- **Quick Checklist** (5-10 items): Takeaway reference guide
- **Closing Thought** (1-2 sentences): Motivational wrap-up

---

## Template 3: "Problem-Solution" (Troubleshooting & Fixes)
**Best for:** Debugging articles, issue resolution, common problems
**Structure:** Problem statement → root causes → solution breakdown → prevention → resources

### Example Structure:
- **The Problem** (1 paragraph): What's going wrong and why it matters
- **Why This Happens** (2-3 subsections): Root cause analysis with examples
- **Solution 1: Quick Fix** (1-2 paragraphs): Immediate temporary solution
- **Solution 2: Proper Fix** (1-2 paragraphs): Long-term permanent solution
- **Solution 3: Prevention** (1-2 paragraphs): How to avoid this in the future
- **When to Call an Expert** (1 paragraph): Situations requiring professional help
- **Related Resources** (3-5 links): Tools and articles to dive deeper

---

## Template 4: "Trend Analysis" (Future-Focused Insights)
**Best for:** Industry trends, emerging strategies, predictions
**Structure:** Trend overview → why it matters → implementation → timeline → expert predictions

### Example Structure:
- **The Trend** (1 paragraph): What's changing and why
- **Why It Matters Now** (1-2 paragraphs): Business impact and urgency
- **Early Adopters Are Already Winning** (2-3 examples): Case studies or proof points
- **How to Prepare** (3-4 subsections): Actionable steps to stay ahead
- **Timeline: What's Coming** (2026, 2027, beyond): Predictions and milestones
- **The Bottom Line** (1-2 paragraphs): What you need to do today
- **Stay Ahead** (1 paragraph): Call to action to keep learning

---

## Template 5: "Checklist & Framework" (Reference & Action Guide)
**Best for:** Audits, checklists, frameworks, strategic planning
**Structure:** Framework intro → categorized checklist → scoring/assessment → next steps

### Example Structure:
- **What This Framework Covers** (1 paragraph): Scope and benefits
- **Category 1: [Topic]** (5-10 items with brief explanations)
- **Category 2: [Topic]** (5-10 items with brief explanations)
- **Category 3: [Topic]** (5-10 items with brief explanations)
- **Scoring Your Results** (1-2 paragraphs): How to interpret the checklist
- **Priority Action Items** (3-5 items): What to tackle first
- **Implementation Timeline** (Quick wins, medium-term, long-term): Phased approach
- **Download & Share** (1 paragraph): Encourage sharing and bookmarking

## Writing Style Guidelines for All Templates

### Tone & Voice
- **Conversational but authoritative** - Write like you're talking to a smart friend, not a textbook
- **Personal examples** - Use "I've seen," "I've worked with," "In my experience"
- **Avoid jargon overload** - Explain technical terms in plain English
- **Inject personality** - Humor, opinions, and unique perspective are welcome

### Structure Best Practices
- **Short paragraphs** (2-3 sentences max) for readability
- **Subheadings every 200-300 words** to break up content
- **Bold key phrases** to highlight important concepts
- **Use lists** (bullets/numbers) for clarity and scannability
- **Include examples** - Real-world scenarios make concepts stick

### Opening Hooks (Choose One Style Per Article)
1. **Personal story** - "I spent 6 months testing this..."
2. **Surprising stat** - "87% of marketers don't realize..."
3. **Common mistake** - "Most people get this wrong..."
4. **Question** - "What if I told you..."
5. **Contrarian take** - "Everyone says X, but actually..."

### Closing Patterns (Author Sign-Offs)
- **Motivational** - "You've got this. Now go dominate."
- **Witty** - "And that's how you avoid becoming a cautionary tale."
- **Curious** - "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]? Drop it in the comments."
- **Actionable** - "Pick one tactic from this article and implement it this week."
- **Humble** - "This is what's worked for me—I'd love to hear what works for you."

## Quality Checklist for All Articles
- [ ] Opening hook captures attention within first 2 sentences
- [ ] Article length: 1,200-2,500 words (optimal for SEO + readability)
- [ ] At least 1 subheading per 300 words
- [ ] Key concepts bolded or highlighted
- [ ] At least 1 example, case study, or real-world scenario
- [ ] Author bio and sign-off included
- [ ] Links to related articles (internal linking)
- [ ] Publication date included
- [ ] Author is one of the 6 team members
- [ ] No grammatical errors or typos
- [ ] Tone matches author's personality
- [ ] Actionable takeaways provided
- [ ] Unique perspective or insight (not generic advice)
